The Journey

This is Texas Tommy

Every player has an origin story. Mine runs from curiosity, through the long grind, to a camera and a sponsorship — and it's still being written.

The short version

A game of
incomplete information

Poker pulled me in because it rewards the thing I can't stop doing — trying to figure out what someone else is thinking. Every hand is a puzzle built out of incomplete information, and the players who win are the ones who ask better questions.

I didn't come up through nosebleeds and TV finals. I came up in the card rooms, one buy-in and one lesson at a time. That grind is the whole point — and it's why the way I teach actually sticks.

Chapter by chapter

Where I came from,
where I'm headed

The Spark

Falling for the game

It started the way it does for a lot of us — a home game, a bad beat, and a stubborn need to understand why it happened. That curiosity turned into hours of study and a decision to take the game seriously. (Edit: Tommy's real origin — hometown, first game, the moment it clicked.)

The Grind

Cutting my teeth in the card rooms

The Bay Area became my classroom — rooms like Bay 101 and Lucky Chances, where the games are tough and the lessons are expensive. I learned to read players, manage a bankroll, and stay level when the swings hit. This is where the craft got real.

The Camera

Sharing the journey

I started filming sessions and breaking hands down out loud — not to show off, but because teaching a concept is the fastest way to truly understand it. The channel grew into a community of players who love the thinking side of poker as much as I do.

The Partnership

Becoming a WPT Global pro

Signing with WPT Global — the official online poker room of the World Poker Tour — was a milestone I don't take for granted. It's where I put in online volume and where my community can sit in the same games with me.

What's Next

Building something bigger

More content, more coaching, and more ways to bring people into the game the right way. The goal has never changed: help players stop guessing and start thinking. If that's you — let's get to work.

How I think about the game

The house rules

Reads over rules

Charts get you to the table. Reading the actual human across from you is what wins the money.

Process over results

One session tells you nothing. Good decisions, repeated a thousand times, tell you everything.

Honesty over ego

The fastest way to improve is to get brutally honest about your leaks. That's exactly what coaching is for.
